E-books related to native spirituality

Reading books related to spirituality is an enriching journey that provides countless benefits. Spirituality refers to the search for meaning and purpose in life, connecting with something greater than ourselves, and exploring the deeper questions of existence.

Tupã Tenondé

In the past, to become a shaman, the Guarani Indian had to know the magic words, the hidden wisdom that only the tamãi (ancient wise men) mastered and were responsible for transmitting. After deep reflection on the teachings, the initiate began to learn the techniques, and then specialize in soul medicine. In the middle of the last century, part of these teachings was revealed to non-indigenous civilization by authorization of a great wise dreamer, Pablo Werá. Now, in the 21st century, the sacred words of the ancients of this land resurface, as they remind us of the interdependence between Man, Mother Earth, the Universe and the Great Creative Mystery – the basis of the learning of the ancient healers.

The Pataxó people and their stories

A book that talks about hunting, forests, rivers, fish, herbs, birds, fields, stars, chiefs, healers... It is the voice of five indigenous teachers from Pataxó – a village located in the municipality of Carmésia, in Minas Gerais – revealing the story to us of its people – habits, beliefs, values, traditions, struggle for survival. The illustrations, also by the teachers, enhance the narrative, accentuating its genuine and original character. And in the presentation of the book, a desire and a hope: Our objective is to build a differentiated curriculum for our schools, with our own reflections and information from our past and future. We hope that this book can return to our schools and also contribute to other non-indigenous schools, to the knowledge of the true history of the country.

The desire of others

In The Desire of Others, Hanna Limulja offers a gateway to the Yanomami world through their dreams. What do they dream about? What does dreaming mean and why is it important? Among the Yanomami, dreams are not the subject's unconscious desires, as psychoanalysis describes: dreaming is inhabiting other worlds, encountering other beings and, in these encounters, mobilizing oneself for the desire of others. Furthermore, the dream, especially when socialized, acquires practical functions, playing a political role in the daily life of the community: dreaming about an enemy is a reason to pay close attention to the surroundings of the maloca the next day; If in the dream the person appears richly adorned, as if they were already in the world of the dead, it is necessary to take care of their safety, to keep them in this world.

Piara Tupinambá

The experience of almost 30 years of research into the science of Numerology led author Luiz Alexandre Junior to narrate his experience with spiritualism and Umbanda and the development of the Tupinambá method. The work celebrates his faith and is another important instrument of self-knowledge and self-healing, which the author shares with us! The Tupinambá path or method, Piara Tupinambá is, first of all, to de-alienate oneself, which in practice is nothing more than thinking for oneself, seeing the world with one's own eyes and in accordance with one's purposes, and not with what is learned and proclaimed by society.

Death and rebirth of indigenous ancestry in the soul

This book presents the works on the Brazilian soul presented at the last congress promoted by the Jungian Association of Brazil, in Bento GonçalvesRS. Several experts talk here about Brazil's indigenous spirituality and how much the ancestral soul was damaged. This work presents a thematic itinerary of what afflicts, what ennobles, what is possible, what has happened, how one can think, how wrongly one has already thought. The objective is to make possible the utopia of keeping this ancestral essence of our soul intact, dignified, pulsating and creative.
