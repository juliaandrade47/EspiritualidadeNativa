Guarani

The Guarani are one of the largest and best-known indigenous tribes in Brazil. Your spirituality is marked by a strong connection with the earth, the search for the Earth without Mal (Yvoty Tenonde) and the practice of tekoá, which means "way of being" in its language. The Guarani believe in divine beings who inhabit the spiritual world and influence their lives. Among them, Nhanderu and Kerana'i are supreme gods, responsible for the creation and maintenance of the universe.
Photo by: Rafael Azeredo / Casa Brasil

Beliefs, symbolism and legends

The central belief of the Guarani is the search for the "Land Without Evil", a place spiritually perfect where there is no suffering. They believe that harmony with nature and the continuity of the teachings of the gods will lead them to this utopian land.

Symbology plays a crucial role in Guarani culture, with elements of nature being incorporated into ceramics, crafts and adornments. You symbols represent spiritual and historical concepts, connecting the tribe with its roots. Guarani legends are stories passed down from generation to generation that teach moral lessons, connect the Guarani with their ancestors and explain the origin of the world and human beings. They often involve gods, heroes and creation myths.

Rituals and traditions

Rituals play a crucial role in the lives of the Guarani tribe, serving as a link between community members, the gods and nature that surrounds them. These ceremonies are sacred opportunities to declare gratitude, seek spiritual guidance and maintain spiritual balance. Body painting is a form of spiritual communication among the Guarani. Using specific colors and patterns, they connect with the gods, celebrate rituals and mark important moments, such as weddings and rites of passage. The Guarani deeply value their ancestral roots.
