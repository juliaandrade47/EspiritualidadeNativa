Macuxi

The Macuxi People have their origins in the Caribbean region or Central America. Currently, in Brazil, it inhabits the north and northwest of the State of Roraima. Despite being found in large numbers in the northwest region, they migrated to other regions through interethnic marriages. In 2004, the Macuxi population in Brazil was estimated at around 19 thousand people and around half of this figure was found in neighboring Guyana, occupying countryside and mountain areas in the extreme north of the state of Roraima and the north of the Guyanese district of Rupununi .

Cosmology and shamanism

The Macuxi universe is basically composed of three overlapping planes in space that meet on the horizon line. The earth's surface, where we live, is the intermediate plane; Below the surface there is an underground plane, inhabited by the Wanabaricon, beings similar to the humans, but small in stature, who plant fields, hunt, fish and build villages. The sky that we see from the earth's surface is the base of the superior plane, Kapragon, populated by different types of beings, including celestial bodies and winged animals, among others, which also live, like humans, agriculture, hunting and fishing. The Macuxi have no relationship with the beings that inhabit these other planes. of the universe, which also do not interfere in their destinies. The intermediate plane, in turn, is not the exclusive domain of humans and animals, but two classes of beings, Omá:kon and Makoi, still inhabit it. The distinction between these two classes seems to have as its basic criterion the place inhabited by each of them. Thus, the Omá:kon category preferentially inhabits the mountains, in particular the rocky and more arid areas of the mountain range, as well as the forests. Their appearance, although very diverse, is markedly wild or antisocial: they have long nails and hair and inarticulate speech. They most commonly manifest themselves in the appearance of hunting animals, although they are the hunters of men.

Their appearance, although very diverse, is markedly wild or antisocial: they have long nails and hair and inarticulate speech. They most commonly manifest themselves in the appearance of hunting animals, although they are the hunters of men. Makoi beings are predominantly aquatic, inhabiting waterfalls and deep wells. As a rule, they manifest themselves under a diverse range of water snakes. They are considered the most harmful beings to men, attracting them to their domain and devouring them. When the Omá:kon and Makoi imprison a human soul (Stekaton), the victim becomes ill and ends up dying. Only shamans (Piatzán) can face the predation exercised by Omá:kon and Makoi, as they have the ability to see them and have supernatural weapons to neutralize them. In fact, the therapeutic action of a shaman – since diseases are evidence of aggressions to the soul caused by these two classes of beings – basically consists of rescuing the imprisoned soul, prevented from returning to the body and that, in a shamanistic session, the chants describe as this action unfolds.

Cosmogony

The Kapon call themselves all Tomba – or Domba, relatives -, in the same way that the Pemon recognize themselves as all Yomba, relatives, similar. The two groups consider themselves related, common descendants of mythical heroes: the brothers Macunaíma and Enxikirang. The mythical brothers, children of the sun – Wei –, forged in an ancient time – Piatai Datai – the current configuration of the world, as revealed by an oral tradition shared by these groups. In several narrative versions – Pandon –, these people say that Macunaíma noticed between the teeth of an agouti, sleeping with its mouth open, grains of corn and traces of fruits that only she knew; He then went out to chase the small animal and came across the Wazacá tree – the tree of life –, on whose branches grew all types of cultivated and wild plants that the Indians feed on. Macunaíma then decided to cut the trunk – Piai – of the Wazacá tree, which leaned towards the northeast. In this direction, therefore, all the edible plants that are found today would have fallen, significantly in areas covered with forest. From the trunk of the Wazacá tree a torrent of water gushed out that caused a great flood in that primordial time. According to myth, this trunk remains: it is Mount Roraima, from where the watercourses that bathe the traditional territory of these peoples flow. The myth thus speaks of the origin of cultivation, which marks humanity, as well as its ethnic differentiation, also expressed in geographic location.
